Timeline: 1–3 Years

You have time to plan well

Your situation suggests care might be needed within the next 1–3 years. That's actually good news — it means you're planning from a place of clarity, not crisis.

With 12–36 months ahead, you can explore options thoughtfully, have important conversations, understand costs, and make decisions that feel right for your family. This window is a gift.

What This Means For You

You're in Discovery Mode

This is the time to get curious. Visit facilities. Talk to friends who've navigated this. Understand what's available in your area and what aligns with your parent's values.

Costs Are Predictable

Senior care costs vary, but they're knowable. We can help you estimate what to expect and explore how insurance, savings, and other resources might contribute.

Your Parent Has Input

Unlike crisis scenarios, you likely have time to involve your parent in these decisions. Their perspective matters, and their comfort with the plan creates better long-term outcomes.

How to Think About This Timeline

A 1–3 year window is ideal for thoughtful planning. Start by clarifying what you don't know: What are the actual costs? What does your parent want? How will this be funded? What resources are available locally?

Then move into exploration: Attend information sessions. Schedule tours. Talk to care managers. Connect with peers who've made similar decisions. The more you learn, the less overwhelming this becomes.

Finally, prepare: Organize finances and documents. Update care preferences conversations. Build your support network. When the time comes to transition, you'll feel ready — not panicked.
